About Impilo

Protecting Childhood. Empowering Futures.

Our Mission

Impilo exists to protect vulnerable children, support families in crisis, and build pathways to a brighter future. We believe every child deserves safety, dignity, and love — and we work tirelessly to make that a reality through legal advocacy, trauma care, and community empowerment.

Our Story

Founded in Johannesburg in 2008, Impilo began as a grassroots initiative led by social workers responding to urgent cases of child abandonment. What started as a small team with big hearts has grown into a nationally recognized nonprofit. Today, we serve thousands of children and families across South Africa, offering adoption services, hunger relief, and emotional support — all rooted in compassion and justice.

Impilo was born in 2008 from a moment of heartbreak — a child left at a hospital with no one to claim them. That moment sparked a movement. A small group of social workers, led by Nomsa Dlamini, came together to ensure no child would be forgotten.

In the early days, we operated out of a single room in Johannesburg, relying on volunteers and donated supplies. Each child we helped reminded us why we existed: to restore dignity, safety, and hope.

Over the years, Impilo grew into a national nonprofit. We expanded our services to include legal advocacy, trauma counseling, adoption support, and hunger relief. But our heart remains the same — every child matters.

“We didn’t set out to build an organization. We set out to protect children. The rest followed.” — Nomsa Dlamini, Founder

Milestones

  • 2008 Impilo is founded by Nomsa Dlamini
  • 2012 First 100 children placed in safe homes
  • 2016 Hunger relief program launches in Gauteng
  • 2020 National volunteer network reaches 150 members
  • 2025 Over 3,500 children protected and supported
